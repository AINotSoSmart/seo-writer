## Maximizing Quality: Which Photos Work Best? 

**The key is optimal resolution.** 
Low-quality images lead to blurry, disappointing videos. 
**Aim for the highest resolution possible.** 

**Facial features** are critical. 
Our AI performs best with **front-facing photos**. 
Side profiles often lose details, making it harder for the AI to map the hug accurately.

Avoid screenshots or heavily compressed images. 
Start with the original files whenever possible. 
**The clearer the input, the more heartwarming the reunion.**

### Handling Group Photos 

Most AI hug generators are optimized for **two people only**. 
If you have a group photo, **cropping is your best friend**. 

Focus on the two main subjects and ensure both faces are clear. 
Remove distractions from the background. 
Extra people can confuse the AI mapping system. 
**Strategic cropping** delivers the best results.

## Troubleshooting Common AI Video Issues 

Don't panic if a video looks slightly off. Most issues are fixable.

*   **Blurry output?** Use higher-resolution source photos.
*   **Digital artifacts?** Try re-generating. Sometimes the AI needs a second pass.
*   **Facial distortions?** Switch to front-facing photos to avoid the "uncanny valley."
*   **Unnatural movement?** Experiment with different input photos. Subtle pose variations change the fluid motion.

### Fixing 'Morphing' or Body Distortions 

Sometimes AI algorithms struggle with clear separation. 
This results in **object morphing**, where two people seem to melt together. 

**The fix:** Use source images where the subjects have distinct outlines. 
A little physical space in the original photo goes a long way in preventing artifacts.
